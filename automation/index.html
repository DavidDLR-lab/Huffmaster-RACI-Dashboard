Headline findings

Rule 1 — classify before you score

A raw RACI mixes steps, outcomes, events, gates, and requirements in one column. Only true process steps get an automation score; the rest are captured and attached. Getting this split right is what stops “Seamless AI validated the contact” (an outcome) from being treated as a step to automate.
Process step — scored Requirement — attach to a step Gate / decision — model as a rule Event / trigger — model as a trigger Outcome — reattach to its step
Worked example — the “source lead” thread. Six raw rows become two clean steps (Download Monthly F7; Load Source to CRM), one outcome to reattach (Seamless AI to validate contacts), one redundancy to confirm (Review Source for Duplicates), one ambiguity to resolve, and one hidden requirement to build in (returning-client history at the point of sourcing). That is the difference between automating the real process and automating a messy list.

Rule 2 — no confident score without a data contract

Each step is assessed on ten fields — ID, input, output, system of record, origin, trigger, dependencies, rule type, frequency, and error-vs-warning. How many are actually derivable sets the confidence flag.
High — ships as a recommendation Medium — system/verb known, I/O unconfirmed Low — little beyond the name → walkthrough
Why so few are High. Inputs, outputs, triggers, and error-vs-warning are simply not in the source RACI for any row — mean data-contract completeness is / 10. That is not a scoring defect; it is the finding. It is exactly what the Phase 2 walkthrough exists to fix, and why nothing ships as committed scope yet.

Rule 4 — four tiers, each carrying confidence

Only true process steps are tiered. A high tier sitting on low confidence is a question for the walkthrough, not a recommendation.
Automate now — deterministic, API-backed, High confidence Assist — human confirms / handles exceptions Not yet — blocked (no API → RPA candidate, or thin docs) Keep human — judgment, physical, relationship

Rules & Guardrails v1.0 — as applied

Rule 1 — classify first
Every row tagged step / outcome / event / gate / requirement. Only true steps scored. Classification is itself inference and carries its own confidence.
Rule 2 — data contract
Ten fields per step; completeness /10 drives a High/Med/Low confidence flag. Inputs, outputs, triggers and error-vs-warning are absent from the source, so most steps are Medium or Low.
Rule 3 — transparent inference
Automatability read from the action verb, whether a system is named, structured input, volume, and implied judgment. A step scoring high only on its name is labeled a low-confidence guess.
Rule 4 — tiers + confidence
Automate now / Assist / Not yet (RPA where no API) / Keep human. A high tier on low confidence ships as a walkthrough question, never a recommendation.
Rule 5 — redundancy & requirements
Duplicate actions and tool-superseded steps flagged for confirmation; requirement rows marked to build into their step.
Guardrail
Nothing is committed scope. Every call is inferred until validated. Low confidence goes to the walkthrough, not the backlog.
